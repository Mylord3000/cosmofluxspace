Gun launch for bulk cargo to orbit.

Demand for mass in orbit — propellant, water, metals, shielding — is growing faster than rocket capacity. Rockets are mostly fuel and expendable hardware; they are the wrong tool for cargo that doesn't mind acceleration.

CosmoFlux builds ground-based light-gas gun launchers. The launcher stays on the ground, is reused thousands of times, and the marginal cost of a shot is gas, energy, and a simple projectile.

Technology

A two-stage light-gas gun: a combustion-driven piston compresses hydrogen, whose high sound speed accelerates the projectile down a long evacuated barrel. An onboard kick stage circularizes the orbit after ballistic ascent.

  • 6+ km/s muzzle velocity class — routine in two-stage light-gas guns
  • Demonstrated basis: Project HARP reached 180 km altitude with a gun in 1966
  • Hardened cargo only — several thousand g during launch
  • Target: under $500/kg to LEO at 10 shots/day
